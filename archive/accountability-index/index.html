The Accountability Index exists to answer a diagnostic question Nigerian governance analysis has rarely answered with method: when something went wrong, how did the system respond? It measures the gap between event severity and accountability response, case by case, institution by institution, sector by sector, and year by year.

Chapters 1–2

What the Index measures

The Index does not measure corruption. It measures accountability failure: the systematic gap between how serious a governance failure was and how seriously the system treated it. That makes it fundamentally different from perception-based governance indices and from general rankings of states or institutions by “goodness” or “badness.”

The specification distinguishes three instruments used by the Archive. The Case Qualification Threshold asks whether something belongs in the Archive at all. The Case Importance Score asks how important a case is relative to others. The Accountability Index asks what the documented case reveals about the system’s accountability performance.

InstrumentApplied whenQuestion answered
Case Qualification ThresholdAt intake, before research beginsShould this be an Archive case at all?
Case Importance Score (CIS)At intake, after threshold passesHow important is this case relative to others?
Accountability IndexAt publication and updated as cases evolveWhat does this case reveal about the system’s accountability performance?
Chapters 3 & 6

Structure and formula

Every case produces two scores reported separately and then combined. The Impact Score measures how serious the event was. The Accountability Score measures how well the system responded. Their difference is the Accountability Deficit. The separate reporting matters because events with the same deficit can imply very different institutional diagnoses.

FormulaAccountability Deficit = Impact Score − Accountability Score. Reporting range: −25 to +25, with very large surpluses capped in aggregate reporting so outliers do not distort system-level analysis.

RangeClassificationInterpretation
20 to 25Critical DeficitThe system almost entirely failed to respond to a very serious event.
15 to 19Severe DeficitVery serious event with minimal accountability response.
10 to 14High DeficitSignificant event with weak accountability response.
5 to 9Moderate DeficitSome institutional action occurred, but incompletely.
0BalancedImpact and accountability response are in approximate balance.
Below 0Accountability SurplusThe system responded more rigorously than the event strictly required.
Chapter 4

The Impact Score

The Impact Score has five dimensions, each scored 0–5, for a maximum of 25. Each dimension must be anchored to a specific primary source. The score is assigned by the Research Director and confirmed by the Editorial Lead at publication.

DimensionWhat it measures
Human ImpactDirect physical, health, or livelihood harm to people caused by the documented failure
Financial ImpactDocumented loss of public funds, economic damage to public resources, or value of public goods denied
Institutional ImpactDamage to the functional capacity or structural integrity of public institutions
Democratic ImpactDamage to democratic norms, processes, and rights
Public Trust ImpactDamage to citizen confidence in trust-critical institutions, assessed by institutional role and nature of the violation rather than polling
Chapter 5

The Accountability Score

The Accountability Score also has five dimensions, each scored 0–5, for a maximum of 25. It is the only score routinely updated as cases evolve, because accountability responses unfold over time. The absence of action is itself a documentable finding, not an editorial flourish.

DimensionWhat it measures
A1 — InvestigationWhether and how seriously the relevant institution investigated the documented failure
A2 — TransparencyWhether findings, evidence, and accountability processes were made public
A3 — ConsequencesWhether named responsible parties faced formal institutional consequences proportionate to the severity of the failure
A4 — Institutional ReformWhether the failure led to documented policy, legal, procedural, or structural changes that reduce recurrence
A5 — Time to ResponseThe weighted timeliness of investigation, findings, consequences, and reform stages

Formal accountability and political accountability are kept distinct. Officials losing elections, resigning under public pressure, or facing party discipline are documented on the case page, but they do not increase the Accountability Score unless formal proceedings also occurred.

Chapters 7–9

Decay weighting and scoring rules

The Index is designed to resist gaming. Investigations announced but never concluded, prosecutions commenced but stalled, or reforms enacted only on paper lose score over time through decay weighting. The point is to document reality rather than reward symbolic motion.

TriggerDecay effect
Investigation opened but no findings within 36 monthsA1 reduced by 2 points
Investigation opened but no findings within 60 monthsA1 reduced to 0
Prosecution commenced but no judgment within 60 monthsA3 reduced by 2 points
Reform enacted but no implementation evidence within 24 monthsA4 reduced by 1 point
Reform body dissolved or defunded within 36 monthsA4 reduced by 2 points

Non-negotiable ruleNo score on any Index dimension may be assigned without a specific primary source cited in the Research File. Where evidence is missing, the score is 0 and the missing source is documented transparently.

Chapters 10–16

Workflow and reporting

Every published case carries a Visual Accountability Profile so readers can see the accountability shape of the case at a glance. The scoring workflow is deliberately separated: the Research Director assigns the Impact Score first, a different editor assigns the Accountability Score second, both are cross-checked against the Governance Outcome Classification and Pattern Detection Tags, and only then is the deficit calculated.

OutputFunction
Visual Accountability ProfileSix-axis visual summary of each case
Living score updatesQuarterly and event-triggered score revisions as accountability processes evolve
Institution-Level ProfilesPublished only once five or more cases exist for an institution
Annual Nigeria Accountability Index ReportSector, institution, state, administration, political accountability, and annual diagnosis analysis
Protected elementsThe formula, evidence-anchor rule, double-blind scoring, decay schedule, and other core rules cannot be changed without full Editorial Board vote

The Annual Diagnosis is the report’s most important section. It identifies exactly where accountability breaks down by combining dimension-level score patterns with Pattern Detection Tag analysis. That turns the Index from a database of scores into a diagnostic map of Nigerian accountability failure.
