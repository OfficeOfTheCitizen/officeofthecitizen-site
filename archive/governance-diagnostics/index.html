Governance Diagnostics converts accumulated Archive evidence into structural diagnoses of Nigerian governance pathology. It is not a summary of what the Archive contains. It is a formal analytical instrument for identifying the underlying conditions responsible for recurring patterns documented in the Archive and measured in the Accountability Index.

Opening Architecture

The three-layer architecture

LayerFunction
Layer 1 — ArchiveEvidence infrastructure. Documented, evidence-grounded cases recording what happened, who held authority, what the evidence shows, and how the system responded.
Layer 2 — Accountability IndexMeasurement system. Scored accountability deficit for each case.
Layer 3 — Governance DiagnosticsStructural pathology. Formal diagnoses of the governance conditions responsible for recurring patterns.

The three layers are epistemically distinct. Layer 1 produces facts. Layer 2 produces measurements. Layer 3 produces structural diagnoses: a more demanding and more consequential kind of claim than either of the lower layers.

Chapters 1–2

The diagnostic claim

Layer 3 asks why the Nigerian governance system produces recurring accountability patterns. A structural diagnosis is not a pattern summary or a political argument. It is a formal claim that a defined governance condition, with a defined diagnostic signature, is responsible for a recurring pattern of accountability failure.

Minimum depth requirementA formal diagnosis requires at least fifteen documented cases exhibiting the diagnostic signature across at least three sectors and at least two administrations. Below that threshold, the finding is a Provisional Pattern rather than a diagnosis.

This depth requirement is not a bureaucratic preference. It is the line between analysis and assertion. A small case set may suggest a pattern, but only accumulated evidence can sustain a structural claim.

Chapter 3

The convergent evidence method

Every formal diagnosis must satisfy five criteria. Together, they eliminate the major ways a diagnosis can be false: sector-specific noise, administration-specific politics, unstable score patterns, untested alternative explanations, or shallow case depth.

CriterionRequirement
Cross-Sector RecurrenceDiagnostic signature present across at least three primary sectors
Cross-Administration PersistenceDiagnostic signature present across at least two administrations
Index Score ClusteringRelevant Index dimensions cluster consistently and differ materially from the Archive baseline
Alternative Explanation ExclusionResource constraints, legal procedure, dataset bias, sector-specific explanations, and temporal coincidence are formally tested and excluded
Minimum Case ThresholdAt least fifteen cases for a formal diagnosis; six for a provisional pattern
Chapters 4 & 15

Publication standards

The distinction between Formal Diagnosis and Provisional Pattern is an evidence-depth distinction, not a quality ranking. Both can be publishable, but they carry different epistemic weight and require different approval processes.

StandardThresholdPublication authority
Formal Diagnosis15 cases, 3+ sectors, 2+ administrations, all five criteria satisfiedFull Editorial Board majority vote and Advisory Board review
Provisional Pattern6 cases, 2+ sectors, 2+ administrations, at least 3 of 5 criteria satisfiedResearch Director and Editorial Lead sign-off
Emerging SignalBelow provisional thresholdInternal monitoring, not published as a diagnostic finding

Alternative explanation exclusion is the method’s hardest requirement. The research team must present each plausible alternative as fairly as possible and demonstrate, using specific Archive data, why it cannot fully account for the observed pattern. Every formal diagnosis must also publish the Archive data that would falsify it.

Chapters 5–14

The nine pathology taxonomy

PathologyCore mechanism
Accountability InversionPolitical status, not evidence, determines whether investigations and consequences engage
Principal-Agent CollapseResponsibility diffuses across long, weakly documented delegation chains
Institutional PhantomismInstitutions exist legally and receive budgets while performing no substantive function
Strategic OpacityWhat can establish individual responsibility is selectively withheld or absent
Consequence DecouplingFormal findings are produced but not implemented in the form of consequences
Reform CyclingReforms are announced, partly implemented, abandoned, and replaced by new announcements
Jurisdictional ArbitrageOverlapping institutional boundaries allow responsible parties to escape full accountability
Accountability DisplacementPressure produces consequences against junior actors while shielding senior authorisers
Capture by ExceptionRegulatory discretion becomes an escape route for actors with sufficient political access

The taxonomy is not exhaustive and the pathologies are not mutually exclusive. Governance systems under serious stress typically exhibit multiple pathologies at once, and their combination is itself a diagnostic finding.

Chapters 16–21

Feedback, reports, and authority

Layer 3 is part of a learning system. Once a Provisional Pattern or Formal Diagnosis is named, new Archive cases become tests of that pattern. The Research Director may issue Diagnostic Research Directives to prioritise cases that would challenge, confirm, or falsify an active diagnosis without lowering evidence standards.

OutputFunction
Annual Governance Diagnostics ReportStatus of all nine pathologies, full diagnoses, provisional patterns, longitudinal evolution, co-occurrence analysis, and governance implications
Trajectory trackingEach pathology is tracked over time as intensifying, stable, or resolving
Comparative diagnosisFuture multi-country diagnostic findings once other Archive countries reach sufficient depth
Authority hierarchyEmerging Signals may be named by the Research Director, but Formal Diagnoses require full Editorial Board vote and Advisory Board review

Diagnostic language is held to a higher epistemic standard than even case-entry language. Structural findings must be evidence-anchored, bounded, and falsifiable. This is what keeps Governance Diagnostics analytical rather than rhetorical.
